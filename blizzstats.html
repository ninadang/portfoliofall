BlizzStats

UX, FRONTEND & BACKEND WEB DEVELOPMENT

OVERVIEW



PROBLEM

The collection of Tableau projects, workbooks, and dashboards created by the Business Intelligence department are published and stored on a Tableau Server. Finding dashboards was confusing to some users who didn't understand how to utilize the server's navigation.

GOAL

Create a web portal for a seamless, personalized experience when browsing and searching for projects, workbooks, and dashboards.

RESEARCH

COMPETITIVE INTELLIGENCE


JasperReports is a server used in conjunction with Jaspersoft, a business intelligence software that provides data visualization and analytic features. The home page of the server presents a list of reports and a side bar filtering system similar to Tableau Server.

+: Clear indication of content type and filters make sense.
-: Lack of visual cues and hierarchy categorization.

QlikView is a platform that also provides data visualization and self-serviced business intelligence. There is a default tile thumbnail layout for displaying dashboards is similar to Tableau Server, but users also have the option to view it as a list.

+: Visually informative thumbnails, ample white space.
-: Suffers from the same navigation problems of Tableau Server--no clear signs of hierarchy or content type.


USER RESEARCH

In a meeting with fifteen members of the Business Intelligence department, I received feedback and suggestions on my ideas through open discussion. Some of the questions I asked them were what features they used, what confused them, and what they'd like to see. They informed me that "Dashboards" is the level they default to and that they rarely use the search filtration. Being able to categorize by the game franchise is something they would want, since that is a common category they use in their work. The idea of a "Recently Viewed" feature was proposed, but it was tossed back and forth between the idea of "Favorites" as a better option. I decided to explore the quantitative data on that usage to decide which feature to implement.

To do this, I accessed the Tableau Server metadatabase, which stores information about the server's user activity. I queried the recent histories and noticed a common pattern of two to four dashboards being viewed repeatedly, in-between others. This pattern followed as I queried further into the past, but the dashboards themselves were different. This conveys the idea that users often reopen dashboards they were previously looking at, but only for a certain time period. A "Recently Viewed" feature would be most beneficial to the majority of the users.

DESIGN

STRATEGY

Changing the internal UI of Tableau Server is not possible due to a lack of open source capabilities. Thus, a custom web portal can serve as an entry point into the server. Based on the user research, new features would include a "Recently Viewed" section, a globalized search function, and categories by game franchise.

SKETCHES & WIREFRAMES

The design of the interface was inspired by the tile layout of Tableau Server and the aesthetic of Blizzard's Battle.net client.

DEVELOPMENT

FRONTEND

Using HTML5, CSS3, jQuery, and Bootstrap, I developed the frontend of BlizzStats. All the user interface elements and the search bar were developed with frontend technologies.

AN UNFORESEEN EVENT

Not being used to developing 8 hours a day (apart from my traditional school days), the frontend implementation was finished by week 6. The original scope was to finish the design by the 12 week span of my internship. For the remaining time, I decided to dive into the backend and see the project to full completion.

BACKEND

Having zero backend experience, I researched different languages and servers that I could learn to implement the "Recently Viewed" feature. I learned PHP and used it to dynamically retrieve and display the recent dasboards for the user.

TESTING

BlizzStats was released to three employees for the first round of testing. After surveying their experiences and fixing concerns, it was released to the Business Intelligence department for more usability tests. I surveyed them after a week of use, and the results affirmed a positive experience.

RESULT

This is a live screenshot of BlizzStats. The application is currently being used at Blizzard Entertainment by departments across the company as an internal tool for accessing Tableau Server.
