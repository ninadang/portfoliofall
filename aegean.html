Aegean Cruises

Information Architecture Project




User Experience Design

OVERVIEW



PROBLEM

The premise of this assignment was to create an excursions website for an imaginary cruise line. Our assigned audience was teenagers (ages 12-18), and our vacation region was set in the Aegean and Mediterranean Seas.

GOAL

The goal we were assigned was to improve the target audience's satisfaction of the excursions they participated in. The excursions themselves could not be changed.

DELIVERABLES

Project Scope, Competitive Analysis, Audience Definition, Content List, Site Map, Taxonomy, User Flows, Wireframes, Paper Prototypes

Project Details

Maria Alberts, Zach Balter, Nina Dang, Jonothan Fuega
Instructor: Karen Morrill-McClure
5 Weeks

RESEARCH

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

We began our research by doing a competitive analysis against three other cruise lines that served a similar region to ours. A website called "Voyages to Antiquity" was the closest match to our criteria--it featured excursions in the Aegean and Mediterranean region. The site does a fantastic job with listing vacation packages and excursions. Excursion descriptions, pricing, availability dates and locations are well presented. "Voyages to Antiquity" does a great job of informing the user what they're getting into, which could contribute to their satisfaction of the excursion. We kept this in mind as one of the design decisions we wanted to implement in our own site.



USER RESEARCH

To better define our users, we divided the teenage audience into two user groups: high schoolers and early college students. Although many demographics and psychographics overlap between the two groups, we identified unique characteristics for both. For high schoolers, we thought about the time they'd have for vacations, which is mainly in the summertime. On the other hand, college students may take on opportunities to study abroad or have gap years. We crafted light-weight personas for each audience group.

DESIGN

STRATEGY

Analysis of demographics, psychographics, personas, and context scenarios led us to consider the elements of friendship and sharing as catalysts for satisfaction. When the audience decides how satisfied they were with an excursion, it is often in hindsight as a memory. Making friends, sharing photos, posting to social media sites, and seeing an influx of reactions from their own personal networks allows the impression to last long after the excursion is over.

RESULT

We designed the site to make it easy to integrate friends into the planning and selection of excursions. The website aims to encourage people to share their experiences--both during and after the excursions.The design of the home page features excursions and a chatting function to the user. The "Featured Excursions" serves as an example and motivator for others to share their experiences, and make the excursions a social activity.
