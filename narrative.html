Narrative

StartUp UW Hackathon Project




UX, FRONTEND WEB DEVELOPMENT

OVERVIEW



PROBLEM

We were given 52 hours to create an application of our choice that would benefit or utilize education.

GOAL

Having two former teachers on our team, we listened to their past experiences about what they wished they had when they were teaching. We narrowed it down to the subject of creative writing--an important but sometimes overlooked subject. We wanted to enhance the writing experience for the author without infringing on their creativity.

DELIVERABLES

Working Frontend Prototype

Project Details

Jennifer Cheng, Nina Dang, Zach Nehrenberg, Grant Timmerman
Project Manager: Vinny Pasceri
52 Hours

RESEARCH

RESEARCH INTO THE SPACE

Searching for creative writing tools that currently exist brought up a lot of applications that were great for inspiration. However, they did not supplement inspiration or provide a platform for immediately putting those ideas into a format. For example, "Lists for Writers" is a well-crafted inspiration tool, but it is missing a platform component:



USER RESEARCH

Due to the limited amount of time we had to deliver the project, we relied on our team members' expertise from their experiences as teachers to develop our understanding of their values. They also called a couple of their colleagues to talk about our idea and ask for their opinions.

DESIGN

STRATEGY

We wanted an application that was easy to use, light-hearted, and inspiring. A major factor that drove the development of our product was simplicity, in which we sought to give the user a small push to get their creative writing wheels rolling. We developed the idea of a prompt combined with a picture to engage multiple senses. This way, we strived to inspire creativity so that no two prompts result in the same story.

DEVELOPMENT

FRONTEND

A team member and I cranked out the HTML5, CSS3, jQuery, and Bootstrap needed for the frontend of the home page and community feed page.

TESTING

We tested our site by running around the hackathon space and telling people to check out Narrative and write a story. We also posted to all of our social media outlets to share the application with our friends and networks, so that we could generate more stories into the community feed.

RESULT

Upon visiting the site, a random prompt paired with an image is generated for the user. The user types their narrative in the text editor and can refer to helpers on the side if they need more inspiration. They can then publish their story to a community feed.
